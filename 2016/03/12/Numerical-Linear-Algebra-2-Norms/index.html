Numerical Linear Algebra 笔记(2) -- Norms

发表于 2016-03-12   |   分类于 数学   |     |   阅读次数

Norms

笔记PDF版可以在这里下载。

1. Vector Norms

A norm is a function $||\cdot||: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}$ that assigns a real-valued length to each vector that satisfy the following three conditions.
$$
\begin{aligned}
&(1)\, ||x|| \ge 0, \text{and } ||x|| = 0 \text{ only if } x = 0,\\
&(2)\, ||x + y|| \le ||x|| + ||y||,\\
&(3)\, ||\alpha x|| = |\alpha|\, ||x||.
\end{aligned}
$$

Examples:


$$
\begin{aligned}
&||x||_1 = \sum_{i = 1}^m |x_i|,\\
&||x||_2 = \left(\sum_{i = 1}^m |x_i|^2\right)^{1/2} = \sqrt{x^Tx},\\
&||x||_{\infty} = \max_{1\le i \le m} |x_i|,\\
&||x||_p = \left(\sum_{i = 1}^m |x_i|^p\right)^{1/p} \quad (1 \le p < \infty).
\end{aligned}
$$

2. Matrix Norms Induced by Vector Norms

$A \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$, the matrix norm can be defined:

$$
||A||_{(m,n)} = \sup_{x \in \mathbb{R}^n\\ x\ne 0}\frac{||Ax||_{(m)}}{||x||_{(n)}} = \sup_{x \in \mathbb{R}^n\\ ||x||_{(n)} = 1}||Ax||_{(m)}.
$$

The $p$-Norm of a diagonal Matrix:

$$
D = \begin{bmatrix}
d_1 & & & \\
& d_2 & & \\
& & \ddots & \\
& & & d_m
\end{bmatrix}
$$

then $||D||_p = \max_{1\le i \le m}|d_i|$.

The $1$-Norm of a Matrix:

$A \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$, then $||A||_1$ id equal to the “maximum column sum” of $A$.

$$
||Ax||_1 = ||\sum_{j = 1}^n x_ja_j||_1 \le \sum_{j = 1}^n |x_j|\, ||a_j||_1 \le \max_{1\le j \le n} ||a_j||_1.
$$

$$||A||_1 = \max_{1\le j \le n} ||a_j||_1.$$

The $\infty$-Norm of a Matrix:

$$||A||_{\infty} = \max_{1\le j \le m} ||a_j^T||_1,$$

where $a_j^T$ denotes the $j$th row of $A$.

3. Cauchy-Schwarz and Holder Inequalities

Let $p$ and $q$ satisfy $1/p + 1/q = 1,$ with $1\le p,q \le \infty$. Then the Holder inequality states that, for any vectors $x$ and $y$,
$$
|x^Ty| \le ||x||_p||y||_q.
$$

4. Bounding $||AB||$ in an Induced Matrix Norm

$$
||AB||_{(l,n)} \le ||A||_{(l,m)}||B||_{(m,n)}.
$$

5. General Matrix Norms

Frobenius norm:

$$
||A||_F = \left(\sum_{i=1}^m\sum_{j=1}^n |a_{ij}|^2\right)^{1/2}.
$$

$$
||AB||_F^2 = ||A||_F^2 ||B||_F^2
$$

Theorem 1. For any $A \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ and unitary $Q \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times m}$, we have
$$
||QA||_2 = ||A||_2, \quad ||QA||_F = ||A||_F.
$$

Reference

  1. Trefethen, Lloyd N., and David Bau III. Numerical linear algebra. Siam, 1997.
